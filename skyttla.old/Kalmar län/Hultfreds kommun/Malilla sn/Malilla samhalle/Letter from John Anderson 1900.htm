RETURN

I Ingegerd Elfströms ägo.

Translation:
Holdrege Nebraska October 21 - 1900

Beloved father and mother

Feel hood!

Now I will take the liberty for a while to talk to my beloved parents. First I will mention that we all are in good health up to now, and hope that these lines will meet you in the good gift of health. It is a long time since I heard from you. I wrote to my brother Axel last Summer but have got no answer, so I hope that you have not forgot me. Something about the harvest here i Nebraska this year. We have had it very dry this Summer so we have got a low harvest this year, but God bless we have got enough not to suffer any trouble. We have enough of everything of what we need to eat and to dress ourselves. We have 75 pigs and we have got corn so we can have them until February when we can sell them if they are not so big we will still make a profit. From wheat we did not get a full harvest, and from oats about a fourth. Potatoes we got what we need, corn a fourth of full harvest. We have fed up 10 calves this year, 3 colts and 75 pigs, and we have 14 horses incl small and grown up, and 20 cattle, 75 pigs. The prices are good for all. Horses, cattle and pigs are very expensive. I now shall end this letter with a dear greeting from us all to the beloved parents and brothers and sisters, yes, Hanna and the children send a special greeting to grandpa and grandma, me too, with all regards your humble son

Johan Alfred Anderson.
